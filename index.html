The Wonders of Beekeeping

Bees are amazing creatures! They live in hives of tens of thousands of bees. As a colony, they care for their young, clean constantly, and gather food. Some of the food sources that they gather are nectar and pollen. Nectar is gathered from flowers, and they create honey out of this. By passing the nectar from bee to bee, the workers begin the process of turning the nectar into honey. After depositing this nectar into a cell, bees will fan the nectar to lower the water content of the liquid until it becomes honey. Cells containing honey are then capped with white wax, so the colony can consume this food source in the Winter.

Different Types of Bees Within the Colony

  1. Queen
    • The mother of the colony
    • Lays an egg every thirty seconds
    • Lives for up to three years
  2. Worker
    • All of the other females in the hive
    • Works within the hive during her early life
    • Becomes a forager bee for the last few weeks of her life
    • Works herself to death in five to seven weeks
  3. Drone
    • All the male bees in the hive
    • Unable to feed himself
    • Kicked out of the hive before Winter
    • Lives until he mates with a queen


Threats to Bees

Bees face many threats. The most pressing threats that they face are varroa mites, pesticides, and disease. Varroa mites are small pests that attach themselves to bees. A varroa mite will lay eggs in the same cell as a bee egg which allows the next generation of mites to attach themselves to the larvae. After that bee emerges from its cell, the new varroa mites are able to live on the bee. These pests are the greatest threat to honeybees as of right now. Pesticides can kill bees if they come in contact, but it takes a lot of contact for an entire hive to be killed. There are numerous diseases that affect bees with varying amounts of danger. Foulbrood is the most dangerous because of its great virulence. Some other diseases are sacbrood, nosema, and chalkbrood.
